Accessibility and respectful cinema etiquette

Accessibility is a fundamental consideration for any public venue, and cinemas in Canada are increasingly attentive to the needs of all patrons. While specific accommodations vary by location, many theatres offer wheelchair-accessible seating, assistive listening devices, closed captioning, and audio description services for select screenings.

If you have specific accessibility needs, we strongly recommend contacting the cinema directly before your visit. Staff can provide detailed information about seating options, entrance accessibility, washroom facilities, and any assistive technologies available on-site. Planning ahead ensures a comfortable and enjoyable experience.

Respectful cinema etiquette benefits everyone in the audience. This includes silencing mobile devices, minimizing conversation during the film, disposing of waste properly, and being mindful of others' sightlines and personal space. For patrons with sensory sensitivities, some cinemas offer sensory-friendly screenings with adjusted lighting and sound levels; inquire with the venue to learn if such options are available.

For broader context on accessibility standards and initiatives in Canada, visit the Accessible Canada Act resource provided by the Government of Canada. This site outlines federal commitments to removing barriers and promoting inclusion across public spaces, including entertainment venues.

We believe that cinema should be welcoming and accessible to all. By understanding your rights, communicating your needs, and practicing considerate behaviour, you contribute to a positive environment for every moviegoer.
